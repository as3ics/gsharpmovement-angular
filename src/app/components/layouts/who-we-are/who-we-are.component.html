What is the Movement?

G# is the last note of a chromatic scale, but it is also the first note of that same scale by being an equivalent Ab. Because of this G# Movement has come to stand for the movement of the overturning of time.

Once in a generation the stars align at just the right time through mythical forces still unmastered by man and there will be but a brief moment where an oppurtunity for an event of pure essence of creation can occur; a moment when something that up until that point in time had never existed in reality, space, time, and possibly even in thought; but then suddenly it would exist, filling entire spaces with its presence. You never know what you are going to create in these rare moments; it could be something good, or it could be something bad. Possibly something really bad. But no matter what it is it always is something truly miraculous, for in escence its existence is due only to a case of a pure true miracle. I believe I have come across one such moment like this in time, and what was created for us before, during, and after the grandiose moment containing all the splender in all miraculouslness is something that is simply too grande not to try and share with everyone that will have it, for its cost was simply "nothing more" at the time to possess, but with future additives of chance, time, pressure, and luck, and the final generation of pure creation is none other than the once in a generational musical production of the indie folk-rock album We Shall Prevail by Gadfly & G# Movement.

Who are these people, one of whom seems to go around naming themselves after some sort of fly? Honestly, it truly doesn't matter a bit at this current time, but I assure you eventually it will matter. And I am figuratively dying to share everything I have learned and all I have witnessed with you and anybody else who will give me the time to listen. I assure you, I will tell all, all in due time.

How to Participate with G# Movement

Music is a collaborative process, and none of the material used for "We Shall Prevail" would have ever been possible without the generous support and contributions of the many individual members of G# Movement, many who have been named but some who have not. Reality is that every single person in G# Movement was originally a stranger to each other at the beginning, and because of this and our belief in spreading positivity in the world through participation in music collaborations we want to give each and every one of our followers the same opportunities to get involved in our actual music creation process as everybody else. Yes, you can, and will have the actual same opportunities to collaborate with Gadfly & G# Movement in their music creating process as they continue their musical journey through life. The easiest way to get involved in the process, and what is by far my first recommendation for anyone who wants to participate in the collective collaborative musical creation process is to either have or create an online BandLab account. Once you have your account I encourage you to simply make a new post where you perform some type of musical talent for you and your fellow BandLab peers. Once you make a post I gaurentee you will get comment and feedback from the BandLab community. Start making new musical connections, which you can do on and off BandLab. If ever you happen to have writers-block or simply don't know what to do on the BandLab app my next suggestion is for you to go on BandLab and spend a little time to find a post by a peer that preferably contains a piece of music that you want to be a part of, or you can even pick an eligible post at random if you like, but you must fork it, and then you must take the time to make and publish your own original contributions to the project. After you have made contributions in BandLab to the collective musical creation process you will almost certainly have posted some original musical content that likely has generated its own feedback on BandLab, likely new user comments from your published posts. If you still are unsure of how to get invloved with BandLab then you are going to have to take the process of getting plugged into the BandLab community slower than most, and I may be unable to slow down and stay connected with you during this adaptation time.


If you disregard the social aspects of the BandLab application and focus solely on BandLabs technical software and online services offerings, you will find that by itself BandLab has created many useful tools and widgets for creating and manipulating audio files. Much of the software BandLab provides of service you can get access to for absolutely free through the library, but I there are now also paid memberships for BandLab, which I believe are mostly focused on providing features of networking, promoting, and expanding oneself, but just using BandLab as a notebook for all your musical projects is mighty convenient. The software provided for MIDI manipulation and the Fx and sounds plugins are always expanding and at this point in time are adequate to take on some large-scale projects using completely only BandLabs interfaces. BandLab might not be winning any sprints in its race to control the music indutry, but with some major current splashes such as buying the magazine Rolling Stones, increased membership, and the continued advancement of their software platform, BandLab has found itself in a position where it slowly becoming an effective player in the competitive musical industry arena that is successeptable to large biases that can drive smaller and medium sized companies out of business overnight. I've Been using BandLab since 2016 and depend on them to not have to spend hours of my time scraping the dozens of hours of many various versions of many multiple projects going back nearly half a decade that I have kept in BandLab safely and securely. I 100% do not work for BandLab, but with a few growing pains, have come to appreciate and respect their evolving software package.

If you don't want to join BandLab.com, you know because all it takes is an email address, there are endless other ways for you to participate in G# Movement. All G# Movement songs are open-source, meaning we will not pursue any infrindgement actions if you use them in almost any way to create your own music to express yourself. We want to spread the magic of music as far and wide as we can. Here are some ways you can let music into your life and experience the joy of songwriting and performance.

  • Fork a song and make it your own
  • Share a project you've been working on
  • Sing like no one's listening
  • Write a poem
  • Try and freestyle for as long as you can
  • Play an instrument with someone else

Gadfly & G# Movement stands for everyone and for an all inclusive society where no one feels left out, alone, or uncomfortable in their own skin. Sometimes just talking to someone is the most important thing you can do; sometimes it's playing screamo-deathmetal songs at the top of your lungs untill you can't scream no more. Who is anyone to decide.
