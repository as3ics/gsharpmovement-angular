What is the Movement?

G# is the very last note of the chromatic scale, but it is also the first note because its nature of being an Ab as well. Because of this G# Movement has come to understand this to be the movement of the overturning of time, which is a never-ending process.

Once in a generation the stars align at just the right time, possibly through mythical forces still yet unrevealed to man, and there will be a chance of a brief moment in time where an oppurtunity for an event of pure creation can occur; a moment when something that up until then had never existed in space, time, or possibly even thought suddenly did exist. You never know what you are going to get in these rare moments, for they are not yet clearly understood, but it could be something good, or it possibly could be something bad. Something really bad. But no matter what it ends up being it is always truly something miraculous, for its existence is only because of a true miracle. I believe we have come across, passed, and are living in one such of a moment like this in time, and what Gadfly & G# Movement have created is something that is simply too grande not to share with everyone that will have it. What I speak of, of course, is the musical production of the indie folk-rock album We Shall Prevail by Gadfly & G# Movement. I hope you've been paying atlest that much attention thus far.

So who are these people, Gadfly & G# Movement, one of whom seems to go around calling themselves after some sort of house fly? Honestly, I assure you it truly doesn't matter at this time, but I assure you it will. Eventually. And we are dying to share with you everything we have learned and all we have witnessed with you and everybody else who will give us the time. All in time, when time is due, you see. This is just the beginning of your introduction but others have already been introduced.


Participate with G# Movement

Music is a collaborative process, and little of the material used for "We Shall Prevail" would have ever been possible without the generous support and contributions of the many individual members of G# Movement, most who have been named but some who still can not. Reality is that every single person in G# Movement was originally a stranger at the some point, but they were brought together through music and through sharing and inviting collaboration with their music, and because of this, and in an effort of spreading positivity in the world through the making & playing of music, we want to give each and every one of you the information so you can have the same opportunities to get involved in our actual music creation process, as many other musicians have in the BandLab community. On BandLab you will have the opportunity to make music with Gadfly & G# Movement in the future. You can find a link to each artists personal BandLab profile in the Connect section of this page, and you are encouraged to explore all of their profiles to learn their individual musical styles, talents, and contributions. Many, if not all, of G# Movement's members know to mark their music forkable, and if you don't like how the song sounds then there is literally an invitation asking you to make it better. It's that simple.


By far the easiest and quickest way to get invloved, and what is my first recommendation for anyone of any skill level who wants to participate in a collaborative musical creation environemnt is to create a BandLab account. Once you have your account I encourage you to start making posts and exploring other artists. Find the people who put out the sounds you like to hear. Once you start posting I can almost gaurentee you you will start recieving comments and feedback from the BandLab community on your musical creations. Now you can start making more connections and start finding artist you have good chemistry with or enjoy making music with, or don't enjoy making music with but god-damned you like the music you make. The opportunities are literally endless, now that BandLab is easily off the launching pad. If ever you happen to find yourself with writers-block, or are simply looking for things to do on BandLab, my next suggestion is for you to spend time and find posts that contains something you want, and Fork it!. Now you can take the time to create and publish your own original contributions based on that project, with access to everything that went into making the song. Either make it your own or develope the original idea to make it better. This is also a great way to meet new people on BandLab, because who doesn't like people liking their music by making more music from the music. If you are still unsure of how to get invloved with BandLab then I just want to assure you that you can't mess this up! Just be nice, because I know you're a nice person, and most people on BandLab are nice too so you have nothing to be scared of.


Even if you disregard all the social networking aspects of the BandLab community and focus just on the utility of BandLab's software and online services offerings you will find that by itself BandLab is very handy & useful. BandLab has created many useful tools, effects, and widgets for creating and manipulating audio files, all online, and all free. There are also now also paid memberships for BandLab, but those are mostly focused on providing social networking features & promotion, but if you just want to use BandLab as a notebook for all your musical projects, or need a MIDI interface it is still very convenient. The Fx and sound plugins are always expanding and at this point in time are adequate to take on advanced & large-scale projects using only BandLab's software, if you wanted to. BandLab might not be winning any sprints in its race to control the music indutry, but with some major splashes such as buying the magazine Rolling Stones, a steady increase in their users numbers, and the continued advancement of its software, I believe BandLab has found itself in a position where it can be an effective player in the competitive musical industry. Almost a house-hold name amongst musicians, but not quite. I think they will be one day.

If you don't want to join BandLab, or can't for some reason, there are still other ways for you to participate with G# Movement. All G# Movement songs are declared open-source, meaning by us we will not pursue any infrindgement actions if you use them in almost any way to either use or create something new from. We literally just want to spread our music & encourage positivity throughm playing and making music with friends. Pursuing music, creatively, is a positive, and productfull way to spend your time with many benefits, and it's not a hard thing to do for people of all music skill levels. Here are a few little things you can do to use music and creativity to feel good and make the world better.

  • Learn a song, but change it to make it your own
  • Get feedback on a song you've been working on
  • Sing loud like no one's listening, even if they are
  • Write a poem, or a letter, just write something
  • Try and freestyle until you mess up
  • Play instruments with a friend
