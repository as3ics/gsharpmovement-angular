FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some answers to some questions you may or may not have.

Yes we are giving away our album for free so that as many people as humanly possible can hopefully share in it as humanly possible. Gadfly & G# Movement would rather have a million people listen to our music for free than a thousand for a dollar each and we openly encourage you to torrent, upload, download, email, zip, airdrop, and not just magnet these files as often and frequently as you possibly can. We would literally pay you to do it for us, if we could.
While many G# Movement members do many different things for money it is the true dream for many of them to one day use their music as the means for doing so. That being said, because no one in G# Movement has relies on music as thier primary income, therefor money, which while nice, is not their primary desire when it comes to thier music ambitions. Each member would still make & play music whether they were getting paid to do so or not, and it's that desire that guided us on our decision. Not money. Getting our music in front of a larger audience is far more desirable than the mere pennies, knickles, or dimes we may garnish for a few streams on the subscription applications.
While the internet brought G# Movement together there have also been some real oppurtunities for members of the group to meet up in real life. In 2017 Gadfly took a roadtrip out East from Michigan to meet up with some band mates, and successfully had a jam session with Matt Mooney at his place in West Virginia. Dom and Gadfly (Zach) are actually cousins, so course they've met, and Gadfly met Chase Holton buying a new phone at the AT&T store, where they would often see each other, and Timmy Jay at a homeless shelter. So yes there have been quite a few real interactions amongst the group. Unfortunately due to some hardships Gadfly had he was away from the computer many times, and sometimes over a year at a time, and this changed the chemistry of G# Movement eventually. But many of the members still make music together outside of G# Movement, and there is still hope that once people recognize this album that there will be some renewed motivation to collaborate and create new music for the old members of the G# Movement.
All the members of G# Movement have a publicly available BandLab profile, links in the Connect section, some more active than others, and in their profile you can access the hundreds of original projects and collaborations that are available for you to fork, modify, and publish at your own disposal. If you make good music we will want to make music with you, simple as that. If you just want to look a song to see how it was made, or to play around with too, that is fine as well. We are used to giving away our music, it's what has allowed us to meet new musicians, and that's why yes, you can collaborate with any of the members of the band you want, nothing is stopping you.
