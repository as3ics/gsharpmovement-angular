Frequently Asked Questions

Here are the answers to some common questions.

Yes, the album is truly 100% free. This album is the total collection of the best of the best of everything Gadfly has ever had to offer as a legitimate album, and it is now free so that as many people can hopefully share in its experience as possible. Gadfly & G# Movement would rather have a million people listen to our music for free than a thousand for a dollar. We openly encourage you to torrent, upload, download, email, zip, airdrop, and not just magnet these files as often and frequently as you possibly can, and we would literally pay you to do it if we could. Too bad we can't directly pay people to enjoy the sounds that are coming into their ears, but one day this may change.
While many G#'ers have done many things for money it is the true dream to one day use music as the means for making it. That being said, no one in G# Movement has relied on music to make their money, and therefor money is not their sole goal when it comes to making music. Each G#'er plays music for thier own personal desire, and it's that desire that has guided each of us on our journey through life with music. Not money. I can't for certain speak for everyone in G# Movement, but I stand to reckon that many of them would agree that getting our music in front of a larger audience is far more desirable than a mere few pennies, knickles, or dimes for some streams.
While the internet brought G# Movement together there have also been some real oppurtunities for members of the group to meet up in real life. In 2017 Gadfly took a roadtrip out East from Michigan to meet up with a few band mates if he could, and successfully jammed with Matt Mooney in West Virginia and some other fellow BandLab musicians at that time. But other than that one trip the music is all that keeps G# Movement together. Due to some hardships Gadfly was away from the computer for sometimes over a year at a time, and the chemistry of G# Movement was not great for some time with a decrease in collaborations and output from the group. But many of the members still make music together outside of G# Movement, and there is still hope that once people realize what G# Movement has already done with the work they did on We Shall Prevail that maybe there will be some renewed momentum to collaborate and create new music for actual group of G# Movement.
